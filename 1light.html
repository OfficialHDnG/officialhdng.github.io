I of IV

Ea lives alone with her young sister, Nyeve. Nyeve is a young adult who often does not come home. Yet now, Nyeve has gone missing for many days.

Before Nyeve’s disappearance, a mysterious boy, Shone, sets up a meeting with Ea.

After the meeting Shone texts Ea saying: “I know your sister has gone missing”. At first Ea does not believe him, because Nyeve is rarely home.

Then, Ea fails to contact Nyeve for the next few days.

Everything Shone has done, including the meeting, seems to be setting up an alibi - or luring Ea out of her home so something could be done to Nyeve. However, Nyeve was not home during the day when Ea went to meet Shone.

Nyeve disappears for many days while Ea searches desperately, and one day, Shone texts Ea again, telling her the concept of “Zeata”. Using the concept of “Zeata”, and predicting the “Zeata” of any criminal who could have harmed Nyeve, Ea successfully captures 3 men who kidnapped Nyeve. Shone was not one of them.

Nyeve was found, having passed away, with evidence of several weeks of abuse.

In the law court, Ea says she wants to release the 3 men. The 3 men are released as innocent.

To the outside world, Ea says she has learned to forgive.

Ea undergoes cosmetic surgery to look different. She renovates her entire home. Nyeve is not home any more; Ea needs her home to look different.

Ea starts studying to become a veterinarian.

Ea changes her name.

To the few people who knew of Ea's existence, they knew Ea was taking positive steps to recovering from Nyeve's death.

But Nyeve's death involved several weeks of abuse.

In her mind, Ea cannot accept the fact that the 3 men who cruelly kidnapped and murdered her sister can just live a care-free prison life. The 3 men like to hurt women. Ea knew that. Ea knew this was part of the 3 men's Zeata. With another "hacking' of the Zeata, Ea successfully recaptures them again by herself.

Ea sets up an experiment, in which the 3 men’s cruel natures end up helping Ea take revenge. Ea does not hurt any one of the men to avenge her sister; the men do that themselves, in Ea’s experiment. Ea, instead, turns into a caregiver who feeds and provides care for the three men. Yet the three men’s abusive behavior never ends, and Ea becomes mentally unable to continue. Ea unlocks her GLYPTIC: The Utopian Fragility.

One evening, Ea takes the elevator to the top of the slum-skyscraper she lives in. On top of a 200+ floor building, Ea jumps off.

In her mind, Ea is wondering: why would a mysterious stranger know Nyeve has gone missing? If the mysterious stranger could provide a hint, to help her capture the kidnappers of her sister, why has the mysterious stranger not done so himself? Did the mysterious stranger know all the cruel things done to Nyeve?

These questions run through her mind, as Ea falls from the top of her building.

Was this the right thing to do?

That was one question she never asked.

For the past weeks now, she has been wanting to do this.
